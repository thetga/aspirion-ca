FDI Reversal Highlights Fragility of India's Investment Attractiveness

Latest FDI data reveal that India's attractiveness as an investment destination is not yet cemented, evidenced by net FDI remaining negative for three consecutive months ending October 2025.

  • The reversal followed the announcement of significant US tariffs against India, causing investment outflows starting in August 2025.
  • While net FDI was strong between April and July ($10.7 billion), subsequent months saw cumulative net FDI decline due to investors pulling capital out.
  • The situation suggests that even robust inflows cannot mask instability when outflows surge, contrasting sharply with the 33% average growth seen earlier in the fiscal year.
  • Concerns arise as domestic companies are also increasing overseas investments, questioning the efficacy of domestic policy pushes like tax cuts and Production Linked Incentives (PLI).
  • This sensitivity to single geopolitical events indicates that positive growth headlines are insufficient; deeper, resilient structural reforms are necessary to solidify investor confidence against headwinds.
